Reiterating from [[What is a drawing?]]: A drawing is a Central Projection on a Plane (1, 2, 3 point perspective) or a Sphere (4, 5 point perspective).

- The center of the radial projection is the **eye**, **camera**, or **viewpoint**.
- Imagine a sphere centered on the camera. This sphere will be called the **Picture Sphere**.  It's Radius is the **Viewing Distance**.
- Imagine also a Rectangle [[Tangent|tangent]] to the Picture Sphere. That is the **Picture Plane**.
- The camera has 3 axes: **X = Right**, **Y = Forward**, **Z = Up**.
- Which also gives us 3 planes: **XOY = Horizontal Plane**, **YOZ = Vertical Plane**, **XOZ = Transversal Plane**.

- The Horizontal Plane cuts the Picture Sphere in the upper half and lower half. The line of the cut is the **Horizon Line**. 
	- The intersection of the Horizontal Plane with the Picture Plane is also called the **Horizon Line**.
- The Vertical Plane cuts the Picture Sphere in the left half and right half. The line of the cut is the **Median Line**. 
	- The intersection of the Vertical Plane with the Picture Plane is also called the **Median Line**.
- The Transversal Plane cuts the sphere in the front half (the one you see) and back half (the one you don't, but it's there). The line of the cut is the **Transversal Line**. 
	- The Transversal Plane does not intersect the Picture Plane.
- The intersection between the Horizon Line and Median Line on the Picture Sphere/Plane is the **Principal Point** (PP). Alternatively, it is the point where the Viewing Direction intersects the Picture Sphere/Plane.
